LipiChakra

A gesture-based keyboard designed from the structure of Indic scripts, not the limitations of QWERTY.

Practice Download Privacy Policy

Why LipiChakra?

Modern keyboards are built around the QWERTY layout, which was originally designed for the English alphabet. While they work for many languages through transliteration or large on-screen layouts, they are not naturally suited to Indic writing systems.

LipiChakra explores a different approach by organizing characters according to their linguistic structure instead of their historical keyboard placement.

How it works

Instead of rows and columns of keys, LipiChakra uses two circular gesture interfaces:

  • One radial for consonants
  • One radial for vowels
  • Character selection through direction and distance
  • Context-aware prediction based on previous inputs
  • Designed for fast one-handed typing

Current Status

LipiChakra is currently an experimental research project. The simulator allows you to experience the interaction model while development of the Android keyboard continues.

Project Vision

The long-term goal is to build an efficient and intuitive text input system for Indic languages that feels native instead of adapting a keyboard originally designed for English. LipiChakra focuses on reducing finger travel while making use of the natural organization of Indic alphabets.
